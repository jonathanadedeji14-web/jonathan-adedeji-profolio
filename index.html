Field sales & cold outreach

SumUp

Door to door selling, cold calling, and reading a room in real time

Active

At SumUp I sell card machines directly to small and medium businesses, mostly by walking in cold. There is no list waiting for me and no warm introduction. I knock on a door, I call a number, or I walk into a shop and start a conversation with someone who has no idea I am coming and probably has thirty seconds for me if I am lucky.

That taught me things a script cannot teach. I had to get good at reading whether someone is actually open to talking within the first few seconds, based on tone, body language, and how busy they clearly are. I had to learn how to ask the right questions early so I actually understand what a business owner cares about, whether that is transaction fees, reliability, or just not wanting to deal with another sales pitch that day, before I try to sell them anything at all.

Face to face objections hit differently than an email reply. Someone can just say no to your face, or worse, ignore you completely while you are standing right there. I had to build real persuasion skills to keep a conversation alive without being pushy, to find the actual reason behind a hesitation instead of accepting the first excuse, and to close on the spot when the moment is right because there is no follow up email to fall back on if I do not.

I carry my own quota with nobody checking in on me daily. I plan my own territory, manage my own time, and handle every part of the sale myself from the first hello to getting the machine set up and working. That combination of cold outreach, quick relationship building, discovery, objection handling, and closing under pressure is essentially the same muscle an SDR or BDR uses every day, just without a phone dialer or a CRM sitting in front of me.

Result: 20 businesses signed up with SumUp's card machine within the first four months, entirely through cold outreach and door to door selling.

Lead Generation System

Cold outreach pipeline, built from scratch

Active

No inbound queue, no purchased list — every lead in this pipeline starts as a name I found and qualified myself.

I also lean on AI to make the pipeline run faster, connecting Claude and a few other AI tools into parts of the sourcing and outreach process to help draft messages, sort through leads quicker, and keep things moving. It speeds things up, but the outreach itself, the actual conversations, the follow ups, and the decisions on who to prioritise are still done by me.

Source (Google Maps / Outscraper) → Filter & dedupe → Outreach (SMS / call) → Track in CRM sheet

Leads are pulled across electricians, plumbers, salons, and gyms in more than 20 Irish towns and counties, filtered down to six valid mobile prefixes and de-duplicated across every campaign so the same business never gets messaged twice.

Result: 10+ businesses signed through this pipeline so far — sourced, contacted, and converted solo, start to finish.
